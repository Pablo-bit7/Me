Under the Hood: The MCP Architecture

To move beyond static spreadsheets, we built a Model Context Protocol (MCP) server. It acts as a standardized gateway allowing Large Language Models (LLMs) to interact with our local datasets dynamically directly inside tools like Claude.

⬇

The Ingestion Pipeline

The engine pulls directly from the three core national silos: SAHPRA establishment lists, the NDoH Master Procurement Catalogue (MPC), and the Medicine Price Registry (MPR). This creates a localized, queryable foundation.

⇄

Harmonization & Normalization

Public datasets often use inconsistent naming for the exact same entity. We implement a strict normalization layer to ensure that regulatory status, tender awards, and private sector pricing are all accurately linked to a single, verified company profile.

✔

Verification Logic

The engine goes deeper than just matching company names. It bridges the link between a company’s manufacturing license and their actual registered portfolio. This critical step verifies if a company bidding on a state tender actually holds the registered dossiers for those specific products.

⚡

Just-in-Time Intelligence

This architecture allows for complex cross-referencing—such as checking a tender winner's regulatory standing in real-time. These operations are executed in milliseconds without the entire dataset ever needing to be stored in the AI's permanent memory.

Current Constraints: Data Throttling

Due to the finite "context window" of current LLMs, we implement "data valves" to strictly limit information passed to the model. While ensuring accuracy, the engine currently analyzes the market in targeted "slices" rather than processing the entire national dataset in a single reasoning step.

Looking Ahead: Vector Intelligence

The move to a Vector Store is the most critical upcoming architectural shift. It will remove current throttling constraints and evolve the platform into a tool for unthrottled, long-term trend analysis.

Current State: Throttled

  • ✖ Limited by LLM Context Windows.
  • ✖ Provides a high-fidelity "snapshot" of the market at a single point in time.
  • ✖ Analyzes data in isolated "slices".
THE GOAL

Future State: Vector Store

  • ✔ Selective Retrieval: Extracts only the most relevant fragments from indexed datasets.
  • ✔ Longitudinal Analysis: Efficiently navigates and compares multiple years of historical data simultaneously.
  • ✔ Scalable Platform: Capable of analyzing trends across the entire national pharmaceutical landscape.
