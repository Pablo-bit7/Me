Current Constraints: Context-Aware Data Architecture

The engine uses a multi-stage approach to work within the finite context windows of current LLMs. Rather than dumping raw datasets into the model, it implements "data valves" — configurable pagination limits (default: 15 rows per tool call) that keep individual responses focused and precise. For broad market questions, the engine's aggregation layer pre-summarises datasets by supplier, molecule, or care level before passing results to the model, dramatically reducing token overhead without sacrificing analytical depth. For granular deep-dives, built-in pagination rules enforce multi-call retrieval so that no records are silently dropped.


The practical effect: the engine analyses the market in deliberate, verifiable slices — trading the illusion of one-shot completeness for genuine data integrity across every query.

Architectural Roadmap: Overcoming Throttling

Moving to a hybrid retrieval architecture, combining semantic vector search with structured aggregation, will allow the engine to answer exploratory market questions across the full national dataset while preserving the exact-match precision required for regulatory and compliance queries.
